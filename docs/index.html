Greenhouse Gas Emissions

Why do we care?

billion tonnes CO2 eq.

Greenhouse gases let sunlight pass through the atmosphere, but prevent heat from leaving the atmosphere. Without greenhouse gases, out planet would be too cold. However, human activities are adding to much of these gases to the atmosphere. An abundance of greenhouse gases causes the atmosphere to trap more and more heat, resulting in the Earth warming up. In 2016, global ghg emissions were 49.4 billion tonnes of CO2 equivalents.

estimated temperature increase by the year 2100

Earth's warming has many negative effects. Warmer temperatures can lead to a chain reaction of negative impacts on people and the environment including lost crops, water and electricity shortages, heat waves, wildfires, pollution, increased spread of diseases, and the extinction of plants and animals. If humans continue adding ghg to the atmosphere at the current rate, the average global temperature could increase by 4-12 degrees by the year 2100.



How do human activities contribute to GHG emissions? Explore the following sections to understand how humans contribute to greenhouse emissions. The Emissions by Sector section provides on overview of where emissions come from. The Food Supply Chain, Energy Use, and Transportation take a closer look at a few of the emission sectors.

How much energy do we consume every year?

Breaking down global energy consumption between countries, we investigate that the average US resdient consumes 10 more times energy than a resident in India and 4-5 times more than a person in Brazil and 3 times of that of a person living in China. Seeing the disparity between low-income, less developed countries, we investigate to understand a year-by-year comparison. Effect

The entire transport sector accounts for 21% of total emissions, and road transport accounts for three-quarters of transport emissions, so road transport accounts for 15% of total CO2 emissions. How can we expect the secotr's CO2 emissions to change in the future? Major technological innovations can help -- such as the rise of electric vehicles. Especially if the majority comes from road vehicles, then energy efficient vehicles will definitely help offset the increase in transport emissions.
