Greenhouse Gas Emissions

Why do we care?

billion tonnes CO2 eq.

Greenhouse gases let sunlight pass through the atmosphere and prevent heat from leaving the atmosphere. Without greenhouse gases, our planet would be too cold. However, human activities are adding too much of these gases to the atmosphere. An abundance of greenhouse gases causes the atmosphere to trap more and more heat, resulting in the Earth warming up. In 2016, global ghg emissions were 49.4 billion tonnes of CO2 equivalents.

estimated temperature increase by the year 2100

Earth's warming has many negative effects. Warmer temperatures can lead to a chain reaction of negative impacts on people and the environment including lost crops, water and electricity shortages, heat waves, wildfires, pollution, increased spread of diseases, and the extinction of plants and animals. If humans continue adding ghg to the atmosphere at the current rate, the average global temperature could increase by 4-12 degrees by the year 2100.



How do human activities contribute to GHG emissions? Explore the following sections to understand how humans contribute to greenhouse emissions. The Emissions by Sector section provides on overview of where emissions come from. The Food Supply Chain, Energy Use, and Transportation take a closer look at a few of the emission sectors.

Breaking down global energy consumption by country, the average U.S. resident consumes 10 more times energy than a resident in India, 4-5 times more than a person in Brazil, and 3 times of that of a person living in China. How do other countries compare?

Transportation

Where do CO2 emissions from transport come from?

The entire transport sector accounted for 16.2% of total emissions in 2016, and road transport accounted for 11.9%. How can we expect the sector's CO2 emissions to change in the future? Major technological innovations – such as the rise of electric vehicles – can help. As the majority of CO2 comes from road vehicles, energy efficient vehicles can help offset the increase in road transportation. If the entire road transportation sector transitioned to a decarbonized electricity mix, emissions could significantly reduced.
